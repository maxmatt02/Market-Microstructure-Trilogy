01 · About

I research the mathematical structure of electronic order books. My work spans three intersecting problems: how algorithmic liquidity architecture bounds high-frequency stochasticity, how institutional price delivery is self-similar across timescales, and how participant order activity scales with capital footprint — the same exponent as biological metabolism.

All three papers are built on high-frequency futures data processed at the nanosecond event level. The Constrained Stochasticity paper covers 8.3 billion CME Globex Level-3 events (ES + NQ, 2022–2024). The Multifractal Delivery paper covers 4.18 billion Binance BTCUSDT perpetual futures events. The Allometric Scaling paper specifies a complete empirical protocol for live CME Level-2 data, pre-registered with falsifiable criteria.

I wrote GPU-accelerated Python for all estimation pipelines: discretized bivariate Hawkes MLE, Baum-Welch / Viterbi for three-state HMMs, MF-DFA generalized Hurst spectrum, and HDBSCAN latency-burst fingerprinting.

Paper 01 2025 · CME ES/NQ Level-3
Constrained Stochasticity in Central Limit Order Books
Regime-Switching Microstructure, Discretized Execution Matrices, and Absorbing Boundary Conditions in High-Frequency Futures Markets
+

Develops a rigorous mathematical framework for modeling CLOB dynamics under the hypothesis of Constrained Stochasticity — the proposition that high-frequency price formation is simultaneously stochastic at the nanosecond level and structurally bounded by algorithmic liquidity architecture at meso- and macro-timescales. Introduces the Discretized Execution Validation Matrix (Φₖ), embeds it within a three-state HMM, and derives closed-form absorbing boundary conditions for leveraged wealth processes. Self-exciting order-flow dynamics modeled via a discretized bivariate Hawkes process. Empirical validation on 8.3B Level-3 events from CME ES and NQ (2022–2024).

  • Three-state HMM: Regime 1 (68.2%), Regime 2 (24.1%), Regime 3 (7.7%). Baum-Welch estimation; walk-forward Viterbi decoding.

Paper 02 2026 · Binance BTCUSDT Perpetual
Multifractal Price Delivery in Algorithmic Futures Markets
Nested Accumulation–Distribution Cycles, Dynamic Hurst Exponents, and Hierarchical Delivery Matrices in Scale-Invariant Order Book Microstructure
+

Formalizes the Fractal Law of Price Delivery (FLPD) — the proposition that institutional order-book liquidity engineering operates as a nested, scale-invariant hierarchy of Accumulation-Distribution cycles across multiple temporal horizons. Three complementary formalizations: (1) scale invariance via Hurst exponent H in a Multifractal Random Walk framework, showing AD cycles across intraday-to-weekly horizons share statistically indistinguishable Hurst signatures (H ≈ 0.62–0.71); (2) the Discretized Multiscale Hierarchical Delivery Matrix (Ψ); (3) the Terminal Macro-Attractor (Ωₜ) as a detectable Hurst-collapse phase boundary. Estimated on 4.18B nanosecond-stamped Level-3 events, 36 months.

Paper 03 2026 · CME ES/NQ Level-2 · Pre-registered
The Network Geometry of Order Flow
Explaining Sublinear Scaling in U.S. Futures Markets via Allometric Theory and Hierarchical Order-Routing Networks
+

Derives α = 3/4 analytically from optimal branching conditions in a hierarchical order-routing network — the same exponent as West-Brown-Enquist biological metabolic scaling — and develops a complete pre-registered empirical protocol for live CME Datamine Level-2 data. Four closed steps: (i) fₖ ∝ Qₖ^{−1/4} from Almgren-Chriss adverse-selection balance; (ii) 2/3 capacity exponent from Square Root Law slippage minimization; (iii) explicit computation of geometric-sum correction C(n,N) showing it contributes M^{1/12}; (iv) martingale robustness to heterogeneous, time-varying branching. Reconciles allometric scaling with the Square Root Law of market impact.

Key Constructs

  • Proof of α = 3/4: Four algebraically closed steps. C(n,N) = n^{11/8}/(n^{11/8}−1) converges at exponential rate in N (<10⁻⁴ for n≥2, N≥10). Effective Terminal Density correction contributes precisely M^{1/12} to close 2/3 → 3/4 gap.
  • Economic corollary: 10× capital footprint → 5.6× message traffic (Y/M ∝ M^{−1/4}). Participant lifespan T ∝ M^{1/4}.
  • Latency-burst fingerprinting: HDBSCAN on 100µs burst feature vectors (latency, size moments, side imbalance, price clustering, time-of-day). Cosine-similarity filter >0.85.
  • IV identification: Instrument Zᵢ = ln(σQ,i/μQ,i). Required: first-stage F > 10, Kleibergen-Paap rk Wald diagnostic, over-ID test if second instrument available.

03 · Code (GitHub)

GPU-accelerated Python pipelines. Data cleaning, estimation, and walk-forward infrastructure. Proprietary signal logic is not published; all statistical infrastructure is open.

hawkes/hawkes_mle.py
Discretized bivariate Hawkes MLE. GPU-parallel conditional intensity estimation. Common-β LR test.
hmm/baum_welch.py
Three-state HMM estimation via Baum-Welch. Walk-forward Viterbi decoding. Regime persistence diagnostics.
phi_matrix/phi_k.py
Discretized Execution Validation Matrix construction. Hadamard composition pipeline. PSD verification via Schur product theorem.
mfdfa/mfdfa_estimator.py
MF-DFA generalized Hurst spectrum h(q). Stationary bootstrap SEs (Politis & Romano 1994). Rolling Hₜ on W=2000 tick windows.
mfdfa/cusum_detector.py
Sequential change-point detection on Hₜ sequence (Chu, Stinchcombe & White 1996). Ωₜ identification and placebo validation.
allometric/latency_burst.py
HDBSCAN latency-burst fingerprinting. 100µs threshold. Cosine-similarity centroid stability filter. τ sensitivity sweep.
allometric/iv_regression.py
2SLS with order-size dispersion instrument. HC3-robust SEs. Kleibergen-Paap rk Wald statistic. First-stage F diagnostics.
data_pipeline/gpu_kernels.cu
CUDA C++ kernels for nanosecond-resolution tick processing. Used for 12.4B event datasets on A100. Binance + CME Globex format parsers.
data_pipeline/wfo_engine.py
Walk-forward optimization engine. 12-month min training window, 1-month OOS, quarterly roll. No-look-ahead-bias verified via parameter roll table.
